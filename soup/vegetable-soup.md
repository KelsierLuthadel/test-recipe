# Vegetable soup

![Vegetable soup](resources/vegetable-soup.jpg)

**Serves:** 6

**Prep Time:** 15 minutes

**Cook Time:** 70 minutes

## Overview
A nourishing, chunky vegetable soup packed with beans, squash, and seasonal vegetables in a light tomato broth. Perfect for using up garden produce, it's hearty yet healthy.

## Ingredients

### Base
- 1 tablespoon olive oil

### Aromatics
- 1 leek (halved length-ways and chopped)
- 1 small onion (diced)

### Vegetables
- 2 carrots (chopped)
- 2 celery stalks (chopped)
- 1 large courgette (chopped)
- 400 grams butternut squash (cut into 2 cm cubes)
- 2 potatoes (cut into 2 cm cubes)

### Seasonings
- 1 tablespoon tomato purée

### Liquid/Broth
- 1 litre vegetable stock
- 1.25 litres water

### Other
- 150 grams dried kidney beans (or borlotti)
- 3 tablespoons flat leaf parsley (freshly chopped)

## Method

### Stage 1 – Prepare beans
1. Put the beans in a large bowl, cover with water and leave to soak overnight.
2. Rinse, then transfer to a saucepan, cover with cold water and cook for 45 minutes, or until just tender. Drain.

### Stage 2 – Cook aromatics
1. Heat the olive oil in a saucepan, and add the leek and onion.
2. Cook over a medium heat for 2 - 3 minutes, until they begin to soften.
3. Add the carrot, celery and courgette and cook for 3 - 4 minutes.
4. Add the tomato purée and stir for 1 minute.

### Stage 3 – Simmer soup
1. Pour in the vegetable stock and 1.25 litres of water, bring to the boil and then immediately reduce the heat to low.
2. Simmer for 20 minutes.
3. Add the butternut squash, parsley and beans.
4. Simmer for a further 20 minutes, or until the vegetables are tender and the beans are cooked.
5. Season well.

## Notes
- **Beans:** Soak overnight for tenderness; canned can substitute but adjust cooking.
- **Vegetables:** Cut evenly for uniform cooking.
- **Stock:** Vegetable keeps it vegetarian; chicken adds richness.
- **Parsley:** Add at end to preserve color.

## Serving
Serve hot with crusty bread.

## Storage
- Refrigerate up to 3 days; flavors improve.
- Freezes well for up to 2 months.
- Best eaten warm.
