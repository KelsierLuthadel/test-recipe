# Deruny

![Deruny](resources/deruny.jpg)
*Ukrainian potato pancakes: grated potato squeezed bone-dry, mixed with onion, egg and a small amount of flour, and pan-fried until lacy-edged and deeply golden. The mainstay of Ukrainian and Belarusian breakfasts and weekday dinners. Soured cream on the side.*

**Makes:** 12-14 pancakes

**Prep Time:** 15 minutes

**Cook Time:** 25 minutes

## Overview
Floury potatoes grate into cold water with a squeeze of lemon (stops browning); a wrung-dry pile of grated potato mixes with finely-grated onion, eggs, salt and a small amount of flour. Spoonfuls fry in butter or oil until each side is crisp and golden — the inside should still be tender. Eat immediately with soured cream and dill.

## Ingredients

- 1 kg floury potatoes (Maris Piper, King Edward; peeled)
- Juice of half a lemon (for the soaking water)
- 1 medium onion (finely grated)
- 2 large eggs (lightly beaten)
- 3 tablespoons plain flour
- 1½ teaspoons salt
- ½ teaspoon black pepper
- 4-6 tablespoons sunflower oil (for frying)

### To serve
- 200 g soured cream
- A small bunch of fresh dill (chopped)
- Crispy fried onions (optional)

## Method

### Stage 1 – Grate
1. Set up a bowl of cold water with the lemon juice.
1. Coarsely grate the potatoes into the lemon-water as you work.
1. Once all are grated, drain through a sieve.
1. Pile the grated potato into a clean tea towel; gather the corners; twist hard over the sink to wring out as much liquid as possible.

### Stage 2 – Mix
1. Tip the wrung potato into a wide bowl.
1. Squeeze the grated onion through your fingers (or in a tea towel) to drain excess liquid; add to the potato.
1. Mix in the eggs, flour, salt and pepper.

### Stage 3 – Fry
1. Heat 3 tablespoons of oil in a wide heavy pan over medium-high heat.
1. Drop heaped tablespoons of mixture into the pan; flatten gently to 1 cm thick.
1. Fry 3-4 minutes per side until each side is deep golden and crisp; the inside should be cooked through.
1. Lift onto kitchen paper; salt lightly while hot.
1. Cook in batches, adding more oil as needed.

### Stage 4 – Serve
1. Pile onto a platter; serve hot with cold soured cream and a generous shower of dill.

## Notes
- **Wring the potato bone-dry:** Wet mixture gives soggy pancakes that won't crisp. The drier you can get this, the better.
- **Floury potatoes:** Waxy potatoes don't release enough starch to bind. Use Maris Piper, King Edward, or russet.
- **Don't make them too thick:** 1 cm cooks through; 2 cm gives raw centres. Spread thin in the pan.

## Storage
- Best fresh; reheat at 200°C for 5 minutes to restore crisp.
- Freezes 1 month cooked; reheat from frozen at 200°C for 12 minutes.
