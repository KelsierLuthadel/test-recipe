# Fiery citrus salsa

![Fiery citrus salsa](resources/citrus-salsa.jpg)

*This unusual salsa, which combines fruit with chillies, makes a fantastic marinade for shellfish and is also delicious drizzled over barbecue-cooked meat.*

**Serves:** 4

**Prep Time:** 15 minutes

**Cook Time:** 0 minutes

## Overview
A vibrant, unexpected salsa showcasing bright citrus balanced with fresh chilli heat and aromatic mint. The combination of orange and apple with raw chilli creates a complex, refreshing sauce that serves equally well as marinade for shellfish or finishing sauce for barbecue.

## Ingredients

### Fruits
- 1 orange
- 1 green apple

### Chilli & aromatics
- 2 fresh red chillies
- 1 garlic clove
- 8 fresh mint leaves

### Finishing
- juice of 1 lemon
- salt and freshly ground black pepper

## Method

### Stage 1 – Prepare orange
1. Slice the base off the orange so that it will stand firmly on a chopping board.
1. Using a sharp knife, remove the peel and pith in segments.
1. Holding the orange over a bowl to catch the juices, cut away the segments from the membrane, letting them fall into the bowl.
1. Squeeze any juice from the remaining membrane into the bowl.

### Stage 2 – Prepare apple & chilli
1. Peel, quarter and core the apple and put it into a food processor.
1. Cut the chillies in half, and if necessary discard the seeds for milder heat.

### Stage 3 – Blend
1. Add the chillies to the food processor with the orange segments and juice, garlic and the mint.
1. Process until smooth.

### Stage 4 – Finish
1. With the motor still running, pour in the lemon juice.
1. Season to taste with salt and pepper.
1. Serve immediately.

## Notes
- **Orange segments:** Cutting away membrane removes bitterness and improves texture.
- **Chilli seeds:** Remove entirely for milder result or leave for more heat.
- **Immediate service:** Flavours brighten when freshly made; does not keep well.

## Serving
Serve as a marinade for shellfish (scallops, prawns) or drizzle over barbecue-cooked meat. Also excellent as condiment for carnitas tacos or grilled fish.

## Storage
- Keeps refrigerated for 1 day in an airtight container.
- Do not freeze; texture and flavour both deteriorate significantly.
- Best served fresh; flavours fade within hours of preparation.
