# Baba Ganoush

![Baba Ganoush](resources/baba-ganoush.jpg)
*Smoky aubergine dip from the Levant: aubergines charred until the skin is black, the flesh blended with tahini, lemon, garlic and a generous splash of olive oil. The smoky character defines it; not just an aubergine version of hummus.*

**Serves:** 4-6 (as a dip)

**Prep Time:** 15 minutes

**Cook Time:** 30 minutes

## Overview
Whole aubergines char directly over a flame, under a hot grill, or roast at very high heat until the skin blisters and the flesh collapses. The flesh scoops out, drains briefly to lose excess water, and blends (or mashes) with tahini, lemon, garlic, salt and olive oil into a smoky, creamy dip.

## Ingredients

- 2 large aubergines (about 800 g)
- 4 tablespoons tahini
- 3 garlic cloves (crushed)
- Juice of 1½ lemons
- 1 teaspoon ground cumin
- 4 tablespoons extra virgin olive oil (plus more to finish)
- 1 teaspoon salt
- A small handful of flat-leaf parsley (chopped)
- 1 teaspoon sumac or smoked paprika (to finish)
- Pomegranate seeds (optional, to finish)

## Method

### Stage 1 – Char the aubergines
1. Method 1 (best): hold each aubergine over a gas flame, turning, for 10-12 minutes until the skin is completely blackened and the flesh feels collapsed.
1. Method 2: place under a very hot grill, turning, for 20-25 minutes.
1. Method 3: roast at 240°C (220°C fan) for 35-40 minutes until the skin is blackened and the flesh has collapsed.

### Stage 2 – Drain
1. Place the charred aubergines in a colander; let cool 10 minutes.
1. Slit them open; scoop the flesh into the colander; discard the blackened skin (a few flecks for smokiness are fine).
1. Drain another 5 minutes — this loses bitter water.

### Stage 3 – Mix
1. Roughly chop the drained flesh on a board.
1. Tip into a bowl with the tahini, garlic, lemon juice, cumin, olive oil and salt.
1. Mash with a fork (for rustic texture) or blend (for silky).
1. Taste; adjust lemon, salt or tahini.

### Stage 4 – Serve
1. Spread on a wide plate; make swirls with the back of a spoon.
1. Pool olive oil in the swirls.
1. Scatter parsley, sumac and pomegranate seeds.
1. Serve with warm pita or vegetables.

## Notes
- **Char hard:** Pale-cooked aubergines give a flat dip. The smoky flavour comes from the blackened skin contact.
- **Drain the flesh:** Aubergine holds a lot of water. Squeezing or letting it drain is what keeps the dip from being soupy.
- **Tahini quality:** Stir before measuring; the oil separates. Cheap tahini tastes bitter; spend up.

## Storage
- Improves overnight. Keeps 4 days refrigerated; bring to room temperature before serving (cold mutes flavour).
